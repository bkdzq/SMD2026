Missing derivation: from SORM integral to Breitung formula

1. Parabolic expression
First, the parabolic expression is obtained as
$$ g(\boldsymbol y) \approx \|\nabla g\| \left[ \beta-y_n + \frac12 \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \rho_i y_i^2 \right]. $$
2. Failure condition
For failure to happen,
$$ g(\boldsymbol y)<0. $$
Since
$$ \|\nabla g\|>0, $$
we have
$$ \beta-y_n + \frac12 \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \rho_i y_i^2 \le 0. $$
Therefore, the $n$-th dimension is restricted by the lower boundary
$$ y_n \ge \beta + \frac12 \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \rho_i y_i^2. $$
3. Joint PDF factorization
Since $\boldsymbol Y$ follows an independent standard normal distribution, the joint PDF can be expressed as the product of marginal PDFs:
$$ \varphi_n(\boldsymbol y) = \prod_{i=1}^{n} \varphi(y_i). $$
4. Express the integral
Now we express the integral as follows: the first $n-1$ dimensions have no boundary restriction, while the $n$-th dimension has the restriction shown above:
$$ P_F \approx \int_{\mathbb R^{n-1}} \int_{\beta+\frac12\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}\rho_i y_i^2}^{\infty} \varphi(y_n) \prod_{i=1}^{n-1} \varphi(y_i) \,dy_n\,dy_1\cdots dy_{n-1}. $$
5. Integrate the $n$-th dimension
For a standard normal variable, we have the property
$$ \int_a^\infty \varphi(y_n)\,dy_n = 1-\Phi(a) = \Phi(-a). $$
Hence, the previous $P_F$ can be written as follows: the $n$-th dimensional integral becomes $\Phi(-a)$, and the remaining part is kept unchanged:
$$ P_F \approx \int_{\mathbb R^{n-1}} \Phi\left( -\beta - \frac12 \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \rho_i y_i^2 \right) \prod_{i=1}^{n-1} \varphi(y_i) \,dy_i. $$
6. Large-$\beta$ approximation
Since we have the approximation rule
$$ \frac{\Phi(-\beta-\delta)} {\Phi(-\beta)} \approx \exp(-\beta\delta), $$
for large $\beta$, we have
$$ \Phi(-\beta-\delta) \approx \Phi(-\beta) \exp(-\beta\delta). $$
Here,
$$ \delta = \frac12 \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \rho_i y_i^2. $$
Therefore, as $\beta$ approaches infinity, we have the approximation
$$ P_F \approx \int_{\mathbb R^{n-1}} \Phi(-\beta) \exp\left( -\frac{\beta}{2} \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \rho_i y_i^2 \right) \prod_{i=1}^{n-1} \varphi(y_i) \,dy_i. $$
7. Take $\Phi(-\beta)$ outside
Now take $\Phi(-\beta)$ as a constant outside the integral:
$$ P_F \approx \Phi(-\beta) \int_{\mathbb R^{n-1}} \exp\left( -\frac{\beta}{2} \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \rho_i y_i^2 \right) \prod_{i=1}^{n-1} \varphi(y_i) \,dy_i. $$
8. Factorize the expression
Now rewrite the expression. Because the exponential and the density factorize,
$$ \exp\left( -\frac{\beta}{2} \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \rho_i y_i^2 \right) = \prod_{i=1}^{n-1} \exp\left( -\frac{\beta\rho_i}{2} y_i^2 \right), $$
and
$$ \prod_{i=1}^{n-1} \varphi(y_i) $$
also factorizes, the integral becomes a product:
$$ P_F \approx \Phi(-\beta) \prod_{i=1}^{n-1} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \exp\left( -\frac{\beta\rho_i}{2} y_i^2 \right) \varphi(y_i) \,dy_i. $$
9. Substitute the standard normal PDF
Substitute the expression
$$ \varphi(y_i) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \exp\left( -\frac12 y_i^2 \right). $$
Then
$$ \exp\left( -\frac{\beta\rho_i}{2} y_i^2 \right) \varphi(y_i) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \exp\left[ -\frac12 (1+\beta\rho_i) y_i^2 \right]. $$
10. Final Gaussian integral
Finally, we have
$$ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \exp\left( -\frac{\beta\rho_i}{2} y_i^2 \right) \varphi(y_i) \,dy_i = (1+\beta\rho_i)^{-1/2}, $$
provided
$$ 1+\beta\rho_i>0. $$
11. Breitung result
Thus,
$$ P_{F,\mathrm{Breitung}} = \Phi(-\beta) \prod_{i=1}^{n-1} (1+\beta\rho_i)^{-1/2}. $$
